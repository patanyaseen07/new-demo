Setup

Draft — Create Problem Statement

The Administrator kicks off the RCA session by creating a clear, specific problem statement. This is the foundation for the entire analysis — a well-defined problem leads to better root causes and stronger solutions.

🎯
Define the Problem
Write a clear, specific problem that the team will investigate together.
👥
Invite Participants
Team members join the session and wait for it to go live.
⏱️
Configure Timers
Set time limits for each phase to keep the session focused.
📌 Example Problem
"Project delays are causing significant business loss and missed revenue targets."
1
Phase 1 — Collection

Submit Root Causes

The session goes LIVE when the admin starts it. All participants independently submit potential causes for the problem. An AI-powered LLM automatically categorizes each cause into the appropriate Fishbone category.

👤 People
⚙️ Process
💻 Technology
🔧 Tools
🌍 Environment
📋 Program
🤖
AI Categorization
Each cause is automatically classified into one of 6 Fishbone categories.
✍️
Auto-Normalization
Spelling and grammar errors are corrected automatically for clarity.
🛡️
Smart Validation
Gibberish, random text, and irrelevant input are automatically rejected.
⚠️
Submit meaningful causes only. The AI validates every submission. Irrelevant text, random characters, personal opinions (e.g., "I don't like this"), and gibberish will be rejected.
2
Phase 2 — Visualization

Fishbone Diagram

All collected causes are displayed in an interactive Fishbone (Ishikawa) diagram. The AI automatically identifies and removes duplicate or similar causes, giving the team a clean, organized view of all unique root causes by category.

🔀
Duplicate Removal
AI merges semantically similar causes to eliminate redundancy.
📊
Interactive Diagram
Causes organized in the classic Fishbone structure for clear visualization.
3
Phase 3 — Consensus

Vote on Root Causes

Participants vote on the causes they believe have the most impact on the problem. Each user gets exactly 3 votes. After voting closes, causes are ranked and the top 2 advance to deep-dive analysis.

🗳️
3 Votes Per Person
Each participant selects the 3 causes they feel are most critical.
📈
Prioritized Results
Causes ranked by vote count. The top 2 move to 5 Whys analysis.
💡
Tip: Vote for causes with the biggest impact on the problem, not just the ones you personally relate to. Think about the bigger picture.
4
Phase 4 — Deep Dive

5 Whys Analysis

The top 2 voted causes undergo a 5 Whys deep-dive. Each participant answers 5 progressive "Why?" questions for both causes — drilling down from the surface cause to the true root cause.

📌 Example — 5 Whys in Action
Problem: "Project delays causing significant business loss"
Cause 1: 🔧 Tools Outdated tools hindering project efficiency
W1 Tools are outdated and slow
W2 Tools haven't been upgraded in years
W3 Budget allocation was prioritized elsewhere
W4 Leadership assumed tools were "good enough"
W5 No feedback mechanism for tool performance
Cause 2: 👤 People Team members struggling with task complexity
W1 Lacking technical skills and domain knowledge
W2 Training programs are insufficient and outdated
W3 Limited investment in continuous learning
W4 Management focuses on short-term delivery
W5 Pressure from clients and stakeholders to meet deadlines
⚠️
All 5 Whys required for both causes. You must answer all 10 questions (5 per cause). The Submit button unlocks only when every answer is filled in.
5
Phase 5 — Consolidation

Similar Whys — Identify Patterns

The AI analyzes all "Why" answers across all participants and groups semantically similar responses together. The top 10 recurring themes are surfaced. Participants then vote on the one they believe is the most fundamental root cause.

🔍
AI Pattern Detection
Similar answers from different participants are grouped into common themes.
🏆
Top 10 Themes
The most recurring root cause patterns are presented for final voting.
🗳️
Vote on Root Cause
Participants vote on the theme they believe is the true root cause.
6
Phase 6 — Refinement

Refined Problem Statement

The most voted "Why" theme is refined by AI into a clear, actionable new problem statement. This connects the root cause back to the original problem — giving the team a precise target for the solution phase.

📌 Example Refinement
Top Voted Why
"Management prioritizes short-term delivery over long-term investment"
↓
Refined Problem Statement
"Project delays persist because management prioritizes short-term delivery, neglecting tool upgrades and team skill development."
7
Phase 7 — Solutions

Submit & Vote on Ideas

With a clear root cause identified, participants now submit actionable ideas and solutions to address the refined problem. Each idea is validated by AI, then the team votes on the most effective solutions.

💡
Submit Ideas
Propose actionable solutions that directly address the refined problem.
🗳️
Vote on Impact
Participants vote on the ideas they believe will be most effective.
⚠️
Submit solutions, not complaints. The AI rejects non-actionable inputs like "I don't like this" or random text. Focus on what can be done to fix the problem.
8
Phase 8 — Outcome

Final Results & Action Plan

The final phase presents the complete outcome. The top 3 most voted ideas are highlighted as priority actions, followed by a complete ranked list of all ideas submitted during the session.

🥇
Top 3 Priority Actions
The three highest-voted solutions are highlighted as immediate action items.
📋
Complete Ranked List
All submitted ideas ranked by votes for future reference and planning.
✅
Session Complete! The team now has a clear root cause, a refined problem statement, and prioritized action items ready for implementation.

FishboneRCA combines the proven Ishikawa methodology with AI-powered analysis to transform how teams identify and solve problems together.
